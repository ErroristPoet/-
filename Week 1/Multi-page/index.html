Streetlight Manifesto

Ska. Post Punk. Ska Punk. Jazz. Rock. Reggae.
These are some of the genres said to describe the sound of this amazing band.
Formed in 2002 out of New Jersey, USA from disparate elements of the Third Wave Ska movement,
this band's sound is heavy on brass, melodic and full of worldly influence.
Once signed into a contract which favoured the record company (a tale as old as time),
and sued for 5 million dollars, this band is not without controversy.
I myself ordered their last album The Hands That Thieve through their personal website,
back in 2012 for its release in 2013, only to have Victory Records (their label)
allegedly refuse to allow the band to distribute the album in this way,
instead preferring bricks and mortar stores and their own website instead (presumably for a higher cut of profit).
This left the band without music to sell, leading to the very interesting concept
of a band's album name directly referencing their situation.
Streetlight Manifesto recommended that fans buy merchandise from their website
to support the band through their difficult time, and provided fans who had pre-ordered their album
through their personal website a blank CD-R with album art CD case and simply left it at that.
Which begs the question; were the fans the hands that thieve
to simply listen to the music, or were the record label simply receiving an unfair share of the credit?
